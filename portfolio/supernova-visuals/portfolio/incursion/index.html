PORTFOLIO
“Doctor Who - The Incursion” is a fan-made collaboration project by Vortrix Productions and Vigilante Pictures which is currently in development. Supernova Visuals is providing all of the visual effects as well as general assistance in post-production. We have created the opening titles and closing credits as well as the various effects shots the project requires such as gunfire, various POV effects, 3D animated TARDIS fly-bys, sky replacements and much more. The story also requires a Sontaran space pod which we have designed from scratch in 3D software, creating our own unique take on the original design.

Opening Titles.
For this shot we were tasked with creating an alien landscape. We have achieved this by doing a series of sky replacements, rotoscoping and adding in various elements to the scene such as clouds, planets in the sky and birds flying past.
Our Sontaran space pod model shown from all angles and a short animation showing how the doors on the pod open.
This is the opening shot of 'The Incursion'. We came up with the idea of an asteroid transitionally opening the episode revealing the 'The Eye of Orion'. The planet and the asteroids were creating using 'Element 3D' then positioned and animated in 3D space.
The first shot in this video is the Sontaran spying and scanning The Doctor and company from a distance using binoculars. The second shot shows the Sontaran watching The Doctor and company from the view of inside his helmet.
This shot of The Doctor's scanner had to be carefully tracked to match the movement of the physical prop. Then we had to digitally emulate depth-of-field on the scanner's display to match the original camera footage.
We added various muzzle flashes, recoil and particle smoke effects to the original footage to make the gunfire convincing. (clip contains temporary sound effects)
This short sequence shows the TARDIS colliding with our custom-designed Sontaran pod releasing energy significant to the plot.
Various shots of our TARDIS model flying through space made specifically for when the story required.
This shot required careful rotoscoping and depth-of-field effects to sell the illusion of the TARDIS landing behind the foreground actor. (clip contains temporary sound effects)
We added several digital lighting effects to make the props illuminate as the actual props did not produce light.
